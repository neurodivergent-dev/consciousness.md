The Phenomenon of Being Strange

Those who are not strange or bizarre cannot understand what it means to be strange and bizarre. From the outside, everything seems simple and superficial. It is unknown what is needed for the wetness of a surface to appear to a person as mere droplets of water. The deepest substance within the layers is never visible to us. Perhaps being strange is like this. It is like being a droplet of water or dust from the outside. A dust particle contains subatomic matter within itself. While the source of the energy required for a person's subjective judgment is unknown, that subjective judgment is lived with. This subjective phenomenon is our way of perceiving. As the world is coded, a multitude of lines are overwritten upon us. Self-copies are the voice of the collective memory, which is considered normal and objectively accepted. This voice is the force that pushes individuality even deeper. The subconscious is like an ocean. The more you drink, the more you drown. The concept of displacement shatters all categories and normality. The individual, who no longer has a branch to cling to, is broken into a thousand pieces. In each piece, meaning multiplies. Spatial position tells us that we are here, in the present moment. It is, in fact, a strange cycle where all positions change instantaneously and judgment systems completely collapse. When the abstraction above all this destroys everything, only a faint, strange trace remains. That trace seems strange to all of humanity. Because as primitive beings, we have learned to evolve and survive. Distances increase. What increases diminishes as it arrives. Silence is the fuel of strange consciousness.
