I Spoke to the Reflection



I was buried in the absurd, I couldn't understand it properly, Am I alive right now, why am I alive? It looked at me, I looked at it constantly. It repeated what I did, I never wanted it.



I asked why we live, it asked me. I said, are you the reflection or am I? It repeated the same thing over and over, echoing. I said, I asked you a question, it said, I asked you a question.



Finally, I punched it, the one looking at me shattered. It fell to the ground, collapsed, my hands were covered in blood, chaos. But its condition was worse, I was sad. I was very regretful for hurting it, I apologized.



Those broken pieces silently apologized to me, I'm sorry for cutting you, forgive me. My heart can't be repaired anymore, I can't put it back together. It said I was a reflection in the absurd, I was exhausted.



I repeated the same thing, it said, I repeated. I didn't understand what was happening, lights were coming out of its glass. Sometimes it's necessary to reflect meaninglessly in this life. All the broken glass came together and rose, embracing me.



"Why did you do this? I wronged you," he said, "for your brokenness, for not being where you wanted to be." He gave me something, I looked at myself, it was a reflection. I held a mirror, and I was looking at myself, lost.



I woke up to find myself inside the mirror, a dimension of nothingness. I screamed as loud as I could, but no one was there. Then I remembered I needed to imagine the mirror. Even closing my eyes didn't make a difference; everything was dark.



I wondered, what was the meaning of this riddle? Mirror, tell me. A light rose, it said nothing, but it spoke. It said, "To reach the truth, one must be cleansed of reflections." I asked, "What does that mean?" It left, it abandoned me.



I waited in the darkness, I didn't know what time was. Was this purgatory? Where was I? What was the truth? I closed my eyes and imagined the universe. At that moment, everything changed. I was in a completely different place; I knew I wasn't inside anymore.



But I wasn't outside either; I was surrounded by mirrors. Wherever I went, I was constantly in a labyrinth. The mirrors altered my perception, as if the room were larger. Each time I bumped into one, it hurt.



I fainted again and woke up somewhere else. I clutched the earth; this time the air was bright. The mirror was gone. I walked towards a tree. Newton was thinking under the tree.



I called out to him, but I didn't recognize him, I didn't know him. "Hey, help me, where am I?" I said. He gave me an apple and said, "Gravity." I looked at his face in astonishment; he smiled at me.



I don't understand. "Yes, you are that person," I said to him. "I am not myself," he said. He had a book in his hand. Its name was Philosophia Principia Naturalis Mathematica. I asked what was written in that book, and he said, "Nothingness."



Nothingness, according to him, was everything. I looked at the sun. And when I turned around, it had disappeared. I walked a little further and came across a village. I observed the villagers' life; a painter was drawing there.



"Good luck," I asked, "what are you drawing?" At first, he hesitated, didn't speak, and showed me his drawing. I think he was depicting peasant life. I stood beside him and watched; he liked yellow tones.



He seemed very lonely. I asked, "Don't you have anyone?" He said, "I have an older brother who's a broker, but he's far away; we correspond by letter." At that moment, I understood, as if I were in the past. He said, "It's unimportant whether value is worth something."



I said, "I don't understand." He looked at me and said, "Never mind, come." I went to him; he lived in a single-room house. He offered me a handful of bread. I said, "You are that person." He asked, "What do you mean?" I said, "You are the starry night."



He didn't understand, but he seemed happy for the first time. He felt valued, he was emotional. But he didn't speak; he had welcomed me as a guest. I had forgotten the mirror for the first time, deeply this time.



Then I woke up in a different place; there were buildings. A person came with something that looked like a circuit. "Did you wake up?" he said. "I was waiting for you." "Who are you?" I asked. "Whoever I am, come," he said.



I followed him and was electrocuted by alternating current. He turned on all the lights, like a time machine. Of course, I knew what it was, but I wanted to ask him, "What is this?"



"A small star in the loneliness of disappearance," he said. At first, I didn't understand why he said that. I'd seen his medal with the letter 'e' on it in his hotel room. I told him he was pure energy; he understood and was silent.



He opened a portal in time, saying matter is insignificant. Is giving it meaning real, or just a reflection? I was surprised; actually, all these people were that mirror. He hadn't gone anywhere; he was trying to show something.



I followed him and jumped into the portal. Suddenly, he transformed into a bearded man, chalk in hand, scribbling mathematical things on a blackboard. He said, "In timelessness, one understands meaninglessness."



At that moment, the equations on the blackboard burst into flames. I watched in amazement. I understood I was following the mirror. The reflection had been changing its form all this time.



I looked at the mirror and said, "Tell me, where am I?" "They are not just you; they are all of us," he said. "To those who wish to be cleansed of all reflections." It was a gift to those who questioned and sought meaning.



I woke up again, looking in the mirror, repeating what I had said. I embraced it with love, I shone, and I brought it out into the sun. This was my mind's book, the place where everything was sunny. I became a mirror among the clouds, reflecting to everyone.
